01 — About

About Me

I chose to study automotive engineering because I was drawn to the technical challenge of how engines work: combustion, thermodynamics, mechanical systems operating under extreme conditions. It's work that demands precision and understanding at a deep level. My undergraduate degree at Manipal Institute of Technology gave me the foundation; my MSc at Oxford Brookes in Racing Engine Systems gave me the specialisation.

More than the coursework, my internships at L&T Defence and DRDO taught me what rigorous engineering looks like. I worked on aerospace components where tolerances were measured in fractions of a millimetre, where small errors could have serious consequences, and where the culture was built around getting things right the first time. That experience shapes how I approach all my work.

My technical focus is mechanical design and systems understanding. I care about the details: how geometry affects performance, how material properties actually compare to specifications, how simulation needs to align with physical reality. My work ethic is straightforward: understand the problem thoroughly, apply the right tools systematically, validate rigorously, and contribute meaningfully to the team's goals.
